It's surreal, this hopelessness that Dain is feeling.

It's pulled him down into the throes of his emotions, past his concerns and into unmentionable eventualities that he's forced to hide from the Red who lies awake and well aware at the corners of his mind. Beyond reasoning and meaning, here, where he's curled into himself there is only despair. He's enshrouded in it, suffocated by this faceless, uninvited feeling that fears neither man nor dragon and has no weaknesses. And, between despair and himself, there is only the harsh reality laid out ahead and how there is no way to plan for it preemptively.

There's no advantage, or insight.

But before facing the future Dain has to  review the past.

He sucks in a ragged breath and staggers where he stands on the dais, body rigid as his eyes pass over his Wing. Most of the cadets are present, some are dead—such is life. It's noisy in the theatre, the noisiest it's ever been, with Professors shuffling to and fro, healers bounding between waves of the mildly injured and stable, even some of the highest ranking available in Aretia peek inside through the heavy wooden door, faces creased and pinched with concern. Dain chokes down another breath and stills himself; Cath allows this show of uncertainty for whatever reason, gentle as ever.

This only serves to make it all worse, and his eyes swarm in their sockets, his vision shutters from his Wing to the stained wooden flooring beneath his bloodied boots.

He doesn't deserve Cath, and he *never* will.

He doesn't deserve the stern reassurance that was pushed into their bond when the wyvern and venin had backed them into a shallow pocket, surrounded by towering, stone townhomes still filled with fleeing civilians. His fists curl tight, knuckles whitening at the press of his fingers into his palm. It's a fierce grip, a punishing closure that makes his arms tremble.

The scene plays again in his mind, again, and again, and yet again.

Dain is there, dozens of meters behind Cath's flicking tail, whistling sickeningly as it slices through the air. He recognizes the signs of fatigue in his bonded, his body flattened to the cold, grey flagstone; his chest expanding and contracting as ragged breaths slip away from his diaphragm; and most notably the worrying that smothers him just before Cath realizes his own panic has seeped into their bond and he snuffs out the traces of it with encouragement.

Cath had held his position fearlessly in the face of his foes but he worried for his bond, his human barely the size of one of his talons. They're outnumbered, they're both exhausted, and in the moment that the wyvern closest to Cath is able to strike it does so just as the deep-marigold of his eyes flick impossibly backwards to find Dain heaving and bleeding against the stone walls.

Marigold and royal garnets are all Dain sees in a sea of sickly grey. Nothing matters but his *Red* and he can't even protect him and the thought devastates him once more, violently. He's shaking, and the force of it summons a healer to his side; some strawberry blonde first-year whose eyes widen when he can't answer her inquiry about his state of wellbeing.

He can't tell her:

*"Yes, actually, this is because I've now realized I'm my greatest advantage biggest weakness. Rather, in a more personal regard, the only person that might actually like me almost died because of me."*

He also can't begin to describe the pain he feels when Cath reprimands him internally, scorching the bond with white-hot fury that makes his shake even harder. So much for shielding; though with Cath shields have always been for naught.

Dain raises his hands, finally—for Amari's sake—and offers the first-year an unconvincing smile, then he slips right back into the mental fray.

He can't *protect* Cath. Dain doesn't have a signet like Riorson, it's not combative, strategic means all is good and well, but it amounts to nothing in the name of protection. His eyes squeeze shut. On Cath's back, yes, his shortsword can filet the underbelly of a wyvern between its scales because he's disciplined and keeps them unnecessarily sharp, because Dain's eyes are quicker than his mind and he can find that point of contention faster than any wyvern could protect themselves.

But the ability to dismantle them with magic?

He doesn't have that, and he needs that. He needs to be like Riorson and Violet, he wants to be a fucking weapon, he can't keep living in this realm of folly where anything besides pure strength and power are worth a damned thing at all. Dain isn't incompetent, he's powerless.

But, it's more than just that.

He's misguided, lost for lack of better word.

He's lonely, friendless in all honesty.

He's tired, and he always is.

He's lived a lie; his future is bleak, and what use is he without purpose.

Suddenly, as the healer coerces another to huddle around him, his body stops shaking.

He's lost, alone, and he's scared.

*"I should relinquish you of this bond then, since your renegade reputation concerns you so, Hatchling."*

Cath's smooth baritone is harsh against Dain's train of thought, it shocks him back to the dais and out of Draithus entirely, and though the swordtail spits the words at him in obvious disgust the bond fills, yet again, with casual reassurance.

*"Can you please stop acting like this isn't a relevant issue?"* Dain hisses right back, instantaneously,

*"That you deign to think I am in need of your protection is sickening; please busy yourself with your position’s responsibilities and not my life."*

Dain scoffs outwardly, and the healers look unbearably confused, *"Were we or were we not saved by another human today?"*

Cath offers something akin to a groan, a draconic rush of air combined with a low grunt, *"I do not recall requesting assistance."*

*"Besides the point."*

*"Do not argue with me."*

The bond levels itself, worked free of Dain's panic thanks to Cath's calm and irritation,

"I'm fine, sorry about that."

The newly arrived healer tilts his head warily, "Sir, pinpoint pupils and visible shaking," he pauses, grips Dain's wrist, "and cold sweats are far past the point of fine."

"Dragon in my head, he makes my eyes like that," Not entirely a lie, "Just remembered seeing a few people die."

Also not a lie, and Dain wrenches his hand away, no longer smiling.

*"You are not fine."* Cath barks,

*"What quantifies fine to you, exactly?"*

There's a moment, and then, *"Hatchling, I grow tired of your sarcastic semantics and moot posturing."*
