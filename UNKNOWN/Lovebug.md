Once they're both accounted for Dain goes to Cath.

Or, more-so, he *flees.*

There's not a reason, not one that he can file away and name easily, discreetly, like everything else. There is a feeling though. One that, since Cath planted his sagging, exhausted body between Dain and an approaching Wyvern, had burned a licking flame through his brain to his heart. The embers catch everywhere and nothing is off-limits; not his desperation to fight and realizing he can't protect his dragon, he doesn't wield a signet that neutralizes a threat. Not his despair; because with his terribly inoffensive signet, Cath is left to fend for both of them. Not his fear, because Cath was going to die for him, though they would go together because a rider doesn't survive without their bond.

Because Dain wouldn't want to survive a life absent of Cath's scorching presence.

Usually he would shield. And his shield's are strong enough to keep his swordtail at the shores of his mind for five minutes and no longer; Cath says Dain should be proud, that Xaden certainly can't keep Sgaeyl out for more than a few seconds. Dain scoffs, thinking of this now as the heels of his boots scrape across the stone floors of Riorson House, because why would Cath know this, and because it comes with the territory of being intinnsic. Of course he'd be able to shield out his own dragon when he's been shielding himself from hundreds of unguarded minds every day for the last year. But, again, it means *nothing.* It amounts to *nothing.* Not after Cath's golden eyes had found him tucked against the city walls, his garnet head swiveled just enough to take in Dain and keep his eyes on the north approaching Wyvern, that sliver of deep marigold just before his pupil shifting and sorting about, braided with emotions Dain couldn't understand without words to accompany them.

In their bond, it felt like *"sorry,"* like an inevitable farewell projected through the open space between them, said softly and bittersweet in the breadth of a millisecond before Cath's second eyelid sweeps over and his attention is back on the threat ahead. There's a lot of words between them left unsaid.

He fucking hates that.

Dain's legs hum, vibrating like the rest of him as he shoves past his cheering peers, happy to be alive another day at the unknown expense of *someone,* or, well no—maybe *something.* Cath's emotions are as scattered as his own and their both so careful in the bond, dipping into the bond, but quiet except for their emotions. So, he doesn't shield. Dain rips himself open for Cath in the bond. He lets his Red feel his *"sorry's,"* his *"I'll do betters,"* his *"what just happened,"* his *"did you feel that?"*

*"I almost lost you."*

*"I can't protect you"*

*"I couldn't save you."*

Cath responds with red-hot anger; emotions so poignant with aggression and ill-tempered, disconcerted skepticism he seems to fucking speechless, which only concerns Dain more, and his feet hit the floor so roughly his teeth clatter with each step. He grits his teeth and sucks in a breath before tearing through the heavy rampart doors at the edge of the House's eastern facing side entrance, and, to his dismay, there are just as many riders and fliers outside as there are inside but Cath is also here, he's flying inappropriately low.

Above him, hundreds are ducking to avoid Cath's descent, civilians exchange screams and gasps of concern, Gryphons duck, riders mutter and their eyes follow Dain up, *up,* *up,* where he sprints to his swordtail on the valley's largest rolling hill. And, that's fine, they can watch.

His taloned feet hang only a dozen or so feet above dispersed hoards of peers, giggling third-years, crying first-years, he glides to an opening a legion ahead, eyes never leaving Dain even when he lands, shaking the whole damn field, nearly throwing Dain from his gait. Cath lands so roughly those ruby-red claws dig holes three times the size of Dain, he flattens his body to the valley floor, sloped against the rolling hill ahead of them, and squares up with his rider.

Dain stops just before his scaled snout.

"I'm sorry."

The words escape past his lips, razing his throat in their wake, breaking off as the sentence ends.

*"Don't be."*

Cath's eyes are on him, sizing him up like they had during threshing. Nothing's changed about his gaze or about him. He's the only constant Dain's had the last three years and Dain almost let him die.

*"Bold of you, to assume that you let me do anything."*

The whooping of celebration continues behind them, but it's quiet where they retreat to in their minds.

Dain surveys Cath's wounds with tight eyes.

*"Fancy yourself another, I do not need your fretting Hatchling."*

Fretting?

Dain openly sneers before dropping his head between his shoulders,

"This is so, so much more serious than fretting."

*"You are right, it is more an annoyance,"*

There's a deep gash expertly placed by some slimy, overcast-colored Wyvern, trailing down the left side of his flank, another across his chest, one right there... right between his nostrils, nearly clean across, diagonally and still glistening fresh with blood,

"You're going to die because of me." Dain croaks; he blinks more, tries to fight it, really, "One day."

*"Hatchling, do not irritate me so,"* Cath's wings pivot upwards and then over, spreading and capturing the moonlight and encapsulating Dain in the veil of their flesh, *"you summon me here to spew this foolishness?"*

Dain shuffles his feet, looks around; Cath has encircled him with his wings in an attempt at privacy, *"I didn't summon you?"*

Cath blinks with both eyelids, *"Yes, you did, what with all of these emotions wailing into the bond. Are they for show? To annoy me further? What is it?"*

"No," Dain steadies his breathing, "I just. I can't—,"

Composure is completely lost on him, he realizes, because he's sweating, fidgeting, digging his nails into his palms. What was the point in even panicking like this? And, so badly that it seems to have genuinely confused Cath.

He could try, honesty. With Cath honesty is good, transparency is reciprocated, though not without sharp teeth and bloody edges. He doesn't need to posture for his dragon; Cath lives in his fucking head for Loial's-sake, and he doesn't belittle Dain when he exposes himself, he's never taken advantage of it. He doesn't disregard him.

Maybe if he had been more honest, he could've been more preemptive, more *perceptive.*

"I was scared." His palms turn upwards pathetically defeat before dropping back at his sides.

There, his first truth. Fear. 

He tucks it into the bond, exploits it where Cath can see it for all its worth and the bond erupts,

*"Of what?"*

"Dying. Of you dying because of me. Losing you." His chin tucks further against his chest.

*"You consigned your life to circumstances that will always be larger than you the second you accepted me."* he flicks his tail towards Riorson House, *"When you came here with that rotted flesh of yours."*

He rolls his eyes at the mentioning of his father, presses onwards, *"Yes, I did, and you?"*

*"I chose you."*

*"Not death."*

*"It means nothing."*

"No Cath, it means *everything.*" Dain lifts an arrogant finger, thrusting it towards Cath's hulking head, "*You* mean everything. To me, at least, you're *everything.* And I almost let you die."

Cath's wings snap back to their standard resting place, tucked against his flank, *"Once again, Hatchling, you do not permit me—"*

Fucksake, Dain groans, eyes stinging, *"You know—"*

*"I am not one of your Wing, whom you may deem—"*

"You know that's not what the fuck I mean, Cath!" Cath lifts his chin indignantly when Dain's voice cracks through the valley around them; he knows he's shouting, publicly, at his dragon, "You know that's not what the fuck I mean."

But...

He's trying to make light of it.

Of love, because, Dain ponders, if that's not what he'd felt during the battle; the fear of losing someone he really, truly loves, this bloodshed and this dismay, and all this fray, and all of the lies, the secrets. The sharp sting, when Riorson's signet all but dismantled what was surely going to be the last thing both he and Cath saw, of despair and dismay and *helplessness.*

They stare at each other.

Dain shakes his head, glancing behind him at dancing torchlight and then back to the Red, he speaks again when his breath evens out and his heart isn't an infantryman beating at his ribs like castle ramparts. He stands up straighter, lets his shoulders sag, and rubs his hands down his face,

*"I can't lose you."* he begins, slowly, *"Not like that... We aren't... peers, or family. I get that. Dragonkind has their ways and humans don't exactly fit into them. But I need you and not because of my signet, or this war, or Basgiath, Cath. You're going to rip me a new one for this but,"*

Yeah.

*"I love you. Really, I do. You talk to me, you know me, I know you care about me even though you bully me for far less than a laugh."*

Cath chortles, vibrating the ground beneath them,

*"Love?"*

Dain nods, *"Love."*

And then, finally, Dain crumples to the grassy floor and cries underneath his Red swordtail's chin.

Why the fuck was that so hard?
